How to Write a
Tech Tutorial

Written By Palvisha Shoaib

So a lot of people I see are always confused about how to write tech tutorials. In my recent assignment
from my class, we had an assignment to do a technical writing and write a tech tutorial.
At first, it seemed extremely difficult to think about a topic, making sure you’re good
at it, and then doing a whole tutorial on it.Today, I’ll share some of the tips that help
me write tech tutorials.

CHOOSE ONE TOPIC, AND FOCUS ON THAT ONLY:

This is the most important part of the tutorial, the topic of your tutorial should be clear
so it is clear and easy for the reader to read and understand. Stay away from broad topics,
and try to narrow your topic so it’s easier to explain.
After you decide your topic, think about why the reader should learn this topic,
what would they create in the tutorial. These questions are important to ask yourself
before you move on. Try to keep things simple. Make sure you know what you’re doing,
before teaching others.

THE CODE:

If it’s a tech tutorial then it would definitely require code. Your code sample has to be
very well-formatted and concise so the reader knows what is happening. Your code plays a really
important role in your tech tutorial, because it exactly shows what you’re trying to make the reader understand.
You need to have clean code to improve the reader’s readability and it needs to be precise because usually
the reader copy pastes the code, so your code needs to be accurate but precise.

THE OUTLINE OF YOUR TUTORIAL:

You want your tutorial to be friendly but formal. Try to not use “We will build” “I will do this”,
try using “You will build” as this sounds like the reader is doing it. Then when you have your code,
and the topic, think about your audience, who is your audience? Is it for a beginner programmer,
intermediate? Be more clear to the reader. When writing the steps, make sure that you provide a brief
description before you put a code snippet, and explain why this is important to do and how this relates
to the actual goal of the tutorial. After you’re done with all the steps, it’s time for the conclusion.
The conclusion should summarise what the reader has accomplished by following this tutorial, you could
also tell the reader what they can do next after learning what you taught them.

SHARE YOUR ARTICLE:

Now it’s time to share your article, make sure to get rid of all the errors before you share it. Find good
credible websites to share your article. Think about your audience, what do you think is a good platform
to use? Is Facebook good? Twitter? If that’s the answer then publish it!
